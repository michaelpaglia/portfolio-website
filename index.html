Background

Most people find systems thinking in a classroom. I found it at 16, building bots to automate sneaker reselling and scaling that to six figures before college. That scrappiness led me to computer science, then to Meta, and now to Fleet.

At Fleet AI, I'm building training gyms for AI agents—high-fidelity simulations where AI learns to use real software and humans provide the supervision. The bet: drop-in virtual assistants that let anyone direct thousands of agents in parallel, accelerating the shift from performing work to directing it.

I also founded 518 Cares, a nonprofit serving the Capital Region—because building things that matter isn't limited to code.

Questions I'm exploring

  • Beyond chatbots: how does AI actually integrate into the fabric of daily human life?
  • How do we capture the tacit knowledge of domain experts—the intuition, not just the answers—to train AI that thinks like a specialist?
  • How can AI bridge the gap between genomic research and treatments for rare cancers?
