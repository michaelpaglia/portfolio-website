About Me

A bit more about who I am beyond the work

I started building bots to automate sneaker reselling at 16—scaling that to six figures taught me more about systems thinking than any textbook. That scrappiness led me to computer science, then to Meta, where I now shape how AI learns from humans.

Today I split my time between two frontiers: leading human data strategy for Meta's superintelligence efforts, and applying computational methods to cancer research at the New York Genome Center. The latter is personal—my grandmother's diagnosis with a rare gallbladder cancer pushed me toward AI in medicine.

I also founded 518 Cares, a nonprofit serving the Capital Region—because building things that matter isn't limited to code.

Questions I'm exploring

  • Beyond chatbots: how does AI actually integrate into the fabric of daily human life?
  • How do we capture the tacit knowledge of domain experts—the intuition, not just the answers—to train AI that thinks like a specialist?
  • Can AI accelerate the path from genomic insight to treatment for rare cancers?
